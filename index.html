What Are Hospital Revenue Bonds?

Hospital revenue bonds are a type of municipal bond issued to finance healthcare facility construction, expansion, equipment purchases, or refinancing of existing debt.

Key Characteristics

  • Tax-Exempt Income: Interest is typically exempt from federal income tax (and often state tax for residents)
  • Revenue-Backed: Secured by hospital operating revenues, not government taxing authority
  • Conduit Financing: Usually issued through state or local health facilities authorities on behalf of hospitals
  • Long Maturities: Often 20-30 year terms to match the life of facilities being financed

Common Issuers

  • State Health Facilities Financing Authorities (e.g., California CHFFA, Connecticut CHEFA)
  • County Hospital Districts
  • Municipal Development Authorities

Key Metrics for Evaluating Hospital Bonds

Metric What It Measures What to Look For
Debt Service Coverage Ratio Ability to pay debt obligations from operating income Above 2.0x is strong; below 1.5x is concerning
Days Cash on Hand Liquidity cushion to cover operations 150+ days is healthy; under 100 days is weak
Operating Margin Profitability of core hospital operations 3-5%+ is typical for healthy systems
Debt-to-Capitalization Leverage level Under 40% is conservative
Payer Mix Revenue sources (Medicare, Medicaid, commercial) Heavy Medicaid exposure = more policy risk
Market Position Competitive standing in service area Dominant market share provides pricing power

Credit Rating Scale

Investment Grade: AAA, AA, A, BBB (Moody's: Aaa, Aa, A, Baa)

High Yield/Speculative: BB and below (Moody's: Ba and below)

Higher-rated bonds offer lower yields but more safety. Lower-rated bonds offer higher yields with more default risk.

Risks to Consider

Healthcare Policy Risk

  • Medicaid reimbursement changes (2025 reconciliation bill altered eligibility rules)
  • ACA subsidy expirations affecting patient volumes
  • Medicare payment rate adjustments

Operational Risks

  • Labor costs and nursing shortages
  • Competition from outpatient facilities and retail health
  • Technology and EHR implementation costs
  • Malpractice and litigation exposure

Sector-Specific Concerns for 2026-2027

  • Rising uninsured rates expected as ACA changes take effect
  • Smaller, standalone, and rural hospitals face closure or merger pressure
  • State-directed Medicaid payment limits affecting certain markets

Higher Yield = Higher Risk

Bonds offering yields significantly above market averages (5%+) often carry more credit risk. Always review the official statement and financial disclosures before investing. Consider whether the additional yield compensates for the additional risk.
